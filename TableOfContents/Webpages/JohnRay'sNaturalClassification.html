John Ray's Natural System Of Classification

John Ray, a 17th-century naturalist, was one of the first to propose a natural system of classification based on the overall similarities and differences among animals rather than just a single characteristic. He emphasized the importance of reproductive methods and anatomical features in grouping animals, recognizing that species with similar structures and reproductive processes were more closely related. Ray also clearly distinguished major animal groups such as mammals, birds, fishes, and reptiles, laying the groundwork for later, more detailed classification systems.
